Compliance Framework

Tax Reallocation Mechanics

Employee funds the employer would withhold for tax are released from tax liability under Section 125 authority. This is not employer money. These are not new employee deductions.

Fund Allocation (IRS Compliance Requirement)

  • • 90% allocated to mental health benefits (required for IRS compliance)
  • • 10% returned to employee paycheck ($35-40/check take-home increase)
  • • Employer saves 7.65% FICA on reallocated amount

Example: $100 Tax Reallocation

  • • Employee would have received: $0 (withheld for tax)
  • • Under Section 125: $10 to paycheck + $90 to benefits
  • • Employer FICA savings: $7.65 per $100 reallocated
  • • Employee net gain: $10/check + mental health access

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this legal and compliant with IRS regulations?

Yes, absolutely. Section 125 Cafeteria Plans are explicitly authorized by the Internal Revenue Code and have been a standard, compliant part of employee benefits since 1978. This is not a loophole or experimental structure—it is codified federal tax law administered through an established provider consortium (Attentive, EmployWell Plus, CP+, Vero).

The IRS sections governing this program include Section 125 (cafeteria plan authority), Section 105 (medical reimbursement), Section 213(d) (qualified medical expenses), and Section 62(a) (work-related deductions).

How does the employer receive a payroll credit without paying anything?

The employer's payroll credit comes from FICA tax savings (7.65%) on the amount employees reallocate through Section 125. This is money the employer would have paid in payroll taxes that is now retained as a direct credit.

Example: If an employee reallocates $10,000 annually through the plan, the employer saves $765 in FICA taxes on that amount. Across 50 employees, this generates approximately $38,250 in annual payroll credits.

Are employees paying for this? How do they get more take-home pay?

Employees technically fund the benefits, but with money they would never have received. Here's how it works:

Without Section 125:

  • Gross pay: $1,000
  • Taxes withheld (20%): -$200
  • Take-home: $800

With Section 125:

  • Gross pay: $1,000
  • Pre-tax reallocation: -$100
  • Taxable income: $900
  • Taxes withheld (20% on $900): -$180
  • 10% returned to paycheck: +$10
  • Take-home: $730 (vs $800 without benefits)
  • Net gain: $10/check + $90 in mental health benefits access

The employee receives a small increase in take-home pay ($35-40/check) plus full access to mental health benefits funded by the tax savings.

Is participation in this plan mandatory?

No, participation is completely voluntary. However, because of the immediate tax savings and mental health benefit access, most employees (94% approval rating) choose to participate. Employees can opt out at any time.

Does this replace our existing health insurance or benefits?

No. A Section 125 plan is not an insurance policy. This is a supplemental "second layer" of mental health and wellness benefits that operates completely independently of your existing benefit packages. It does not affect, replace, or modify any current policies. Your existing health insurance, dental, vision, and other benefits remain unchanged.

What mental health benefits do employees receive?

Employees receive comprehensive mental health and wellness access including:

  • 6 personal therapy sessions per year ($0 copay)
  • 4 couples therapy sessions per year ($0 copay)
  • Mayo Clinic mental health programs
  • Addiction recovery programs
  • Wellness portal (breathwork, meditation, yoga, nutrition)
  • Identity protection for employee and family
  • Monthly wellness codes (100% off products/courses)

How quickly do the savings and benefits start?

The savings for both employees and employers begin immediately. Payroll tax reductions are reflected in the first paycheck after plan implementation. Mental health benefit access activates simultaneously with the first tax reallocation.

Is this a "use-it-or-lose-it" plan? Do benefits carry over year to year?

Benefits are available monthly and do not carry over from one calendar year to the next. However, because benefits are accessible every month (not just once per year), employees have consistent access and do not face a year-end "lose it" scenario. This is a monthly access model, not an annual accumulation model.
